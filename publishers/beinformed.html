Be InformedAfricanBooks.com

Author / Publishing Agreements

A book publishing agreement is a legally binding agreement between an author of a book and a book publisher which specifies all the terms of their deal in bringing a book to publish. These agreements almost always include the details of the parties involved, a description of the work, a grant of rights, manuscript specifications, copyright ownership information, indemnity, publication details, payment agreements, advertising and promotion guidelines, among others.

All publishers, wishing to sell their author/client’s work on AfricanBooks.com must upload a scanned copy of their signed agreement which clearly stipulates their right to promote and sell the book they have uploaded to our platform. The agreement must also clearly outline the author payment agreement between the publisher and author, and should match with the author/publisher banking information provided to AfricanBooks.com. In most cases, authors maintain the copyright over their books, and as such, AfricanBooks.com will pay the majority of the royalties to the copyright holder.

Please note, that publishers without a publishing agreement will not be allowed to publish a book on our site without the express written permission of the author and/or legal copyright holder of the work in question.

Anti-Plagarism Policy

AfricanBooks.com takes plagiarism very seriously. Plagiarism is defined as using someone else’s work without proper citation or consent. This includes the unlawful copying and/reproduction of substantial portions of a source text. Authors must be mindful to attribute the source documents within their works using footnotes, endnotes or some other method to give credit to another person’s ideas or publications.

Authors and publishers found to be publishing work which does not belong to them will have their books removed from our platform. Authors and/or publishers who are found to consistently plagiarize or subvert the intellectual copyrights of others may have their accounts closed and may face legal prosecution from the content’s legal copyright holders.

ISBN Numbers

We require that all books published on AfricanBooks.com have an ISBN Number. This is an international number assigned to your book and often used on the barcodes of your back cover. Depending on your country of writing, you may or may not be issued a unique eBook ISBN Number. US Law requires that every format of a book have its own ISBN Number therefore books originating from the US would need different ISBNs for both your print and/or eBook. However, other nations, such as Uganda only issue an ISBN for differing book titles, therefore you would use the same ISBN on both the print and eBook formats of the same title book.

When uploading your book for publication, you will be required to include the ISBN Number under the book details section. You can verify that your book is properly registered by cross checking your ISBN against the title in an online ISBN Checker. When the ISBN Number is submitted your name and book information should appear, if the ISBN is properly done. AfricanBooks.com has the right to reject any book for publication that does not have a verifiable ISBN Number.

Copyright Protection

Every author needs to copyright their book to ensure maximum security of their intellectual property. Copyright protection varies from country-to-country however. Your best source of information is from your country’s National Library. In most cases, the National Library will have you submit a copyright registration form, several copies of your printed work, along with a copyright registration fee to process your copyright.

Copyright pages generally appear at the front of your book, immediately following the title page and before the table of contents. Your copyright page should include the following essential information:

The copyright symbol ©. This symbol should be followed by the date your book was released and/or copyrighted and the name of the person who holds the copyright. For example; ©2015 Jane Doe.
You must include the year of publication of the book
The name of the owner of the copyright. Usually this is the author or the publisher of the work.
Copyright Statement/Notice
Reservation of rights
The Book editions of the particular work
The Book’s ISBN Number
Credits to the book; i.e.: Book designer, editor, publisher, etc.
Disclaimer (releasing yourself from the risk of lawsuits based on the opinions, statements, or characters within the book)
Ordering or contact information

Intellectual Property Rights

AfricanBooks.com requires that you hold the publishing rights to any content you upload for sale on our site. Uploading or attempting to upload any content for which you do not have rights can result in the rejection or removal of your book, negative impact to your account status, and/or loss of royalties.

This page provides information about intellectual property (IP) rights and common IP concerns that might arise when publishing your books through AfricanBooks.com. This is not legal advice. You should consult a lawyer if you have a specific question about your IP rights or the IP rights of others.

AfricanBooks.com respects the intellectual property of others. If you believe that your work has been copied in a way that constitutes copyright infringement, please notify us of the work in question at admin@africanbooks.com

For more information on Intellectual Property rights and our stance as AfricanBooks.com, please read our full policy statement.
