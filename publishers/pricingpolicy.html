Pricing PolicyAfricanBooks.com

Royalties

Royalties are applied to the sales of all eBook and audiobooks sold to customers within the AfricanBooks.com community.

Royalties are paid to Authors and/or Publishers with an active AfricanBooks.com account

Calculations

Royalties are calculated as (Royalty % X Sale Price) = Royalty.

Taxes

VAT and any applicable taxes will be paid by the author/publisher from the royalties they earn as independent sellers who use AfricanBooks.com as their publishing platform of choice, and not employees of AfricanBooks.com. Where required to do so by law, AfricanBooks.com will report the royalty earnings of its authors to the appropriate tax governing body in the country in which the author resides. In countries that require AfricanBooks.com to withhold taxes before distributing author/publisher royalties, the calculation of the royalty will be as follows:

Royalty % x (sale price – applicable tax) = Royalties

For authors with both a publisher and author royalty, the royalty will be split into 2 payment percentages as seen in the chart below. Both the author and publisher will be sent royalties from AfricanBooks.com. In instances were an author has a private agreement with a co-author or additional publisher, the author is required to distribute royalties to the other party agreements from the royalties distributed to them from AfricanBooks.com and in accordance with all governing tax requirements applicable to them.

Payments & Fees

Authors and publishers can request a royalty payout on any accumulated royalties valued at or above $30. A payout request can be initiated when the author or publisher is logged into their publishing platform. Any collection or distribution fees incurred by AfricanBooks.com on behalf of an author’s sold books will be deducted from their overall royalties using the following calculation:

Royalty % x (sale price – fees) = Royalties

List Price

The author/publisher will provide AfricanBooks.com with the list price of each book you make available for sale. Sales tax, VAT (Value Added Tax) and GST (Goods and Services Taxes) may be added to your list price dependent upon the buyer/seller locations and the governing law pertaining to each particular sale.

Price Range

For eBooks/audiobooks to be accepting in our publishing program, list prices must be within the minimum/maximum price ranges listed below.

Please note, books over the specified file size requirements will garner an additional delivery charge on each sale to accommodate the storage and delivery associated with larger files. As a general rule, we change $0.15 per megabyte (1MB) when your book files is over your allotted file size allowance. If the 35% of 33+2% royalty scheme is selected, we will not deduct delivery charges unless the book is listed between: $0.99-2.99. In such cases, files should be 3mbs or less to avoid delivery charges.

Account Standing

Authors and publishers are required to keep their accounts and payment information updated. AfricanBooks.com cannot pay royalties to accounts without valid payment information and verifiable tax details. In such cases, AfricanBooks.com will retain author royalties until payment information is updated.

Similarly, AfricanBooks.com can only pay authors and publishers accounts in clear standing. Any account with suspected Intellectual property rights (IP) or fraudulent activity will have their royalties withheld until the matter can be cleared and the rightful owner of the royalties verified.

Program Policies

This policy works in conjunction with any other publishing or program policies issued by AfricanBooks.com. In circumstances where policy information conflicts, the pricing policy will have the final say on list pricing and royalties, while the program policy will have the final say on program related matters.

Changes

Authors can change their royalty options and list prices at any time under the bookshelf tab, manage books, section on their dashboard. Changes will go into effect immediately on our store.

AfricanBooks.com has the ultimate right to change the list price of a book as well as a Payment Plan for any author/book as it deems fit, in order to spur sales or to account for larger file sizes.

Royalty schemes maybe changed and updated from time to time. Author/publishers continual use of the program will be counted as acceptance to the changes.
