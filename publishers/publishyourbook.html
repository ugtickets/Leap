Publish Your BookAfricanBooks.com

Creating a Book Sample

We encourage authors to create a book sample to give readers a sneak peak of what they will get if they purchase your book. This is similar to a reader in a bookstore who might pick up your book and flip through the pages before they walk to the cashier to purchase it.

Book samples should include your book’s front cover, copyright information, book introduction, table of contents and back cover at the very minimum. If you would like to include a bit more, you can include the first chapter of your book for readers to read, however this is not mandatory.

We encourage authors and publishers to only include about 10% of your book in a sample, meaning, if your book is 100 pages, include 10 pages or less as a free sample. Likewise, if your book is 200 pages, consider giving readers about 20 pages of a free sample. It is proven that samples encourage and entice people to buy your book.

Formatting Your eBooks

We currently accept eBooks in .epub or .pdf format. Your book should have adequate line spacing, page borders and font sizing for easy readability. Just like the interior formatting of a print book is important, the interior format of your eBooks is equally important for a good reading experience.

We encourage authors and publishers to keep the interior files of their books under 5mbs if possible. Books over 5mbs in content will be required to pay a ‘delivery’ charge when they sell their books. This delivery charge will be deducted from the author’s royalties with every sale. The delivery charge covers the extra cost incurred by AfricanBooks.com to host, store and send larger files on our servers. It is important to note, that if you are selling books with large digital images and illustrations, that you should not compromise the quality of the interior artwork over the overall size of the file.

Formatting Your Audiobooks

For authors and publishers with .mp3 formatted audiobooks, it is very important to ensure your audiobook has been recorded with clear, high-quality sound, with no background noise. We recommend you professionally record your audiobooks, using a reader who has a clear and understandable accent. The reading should be smooth and flow without stuttering, pitch adjustments, etc. We recommend that you divide your .mp3’s into chapters, providing one .mp3 file for each chapter, clearly labeling the chapter # on the recording. Audiobook files will be reviewed for clarity before they will be approved for distribution by the AfricanBooks.com team.

Book Cover Requirements

All eBook and audiobooks must have a high-quality cover. Your cover should be uploaded separately as a .pdf, .eps, or .jpg file. The cover art should be large enough to prevent pixelating on the AfricanBooks.com website. Pixelated covers will be rejected and need to be uploaded again before your book can be approved for publishing. Cover art should be sized ______ x _______, with a minimum file size of ______ and a maximum file size of ________.

Cover art should only include the front cover without the spine or back cover. If you wish to include your back cover art, the back cover should be included in your interior content file as the last page of your file.

Your cover art should include the book’s title, subtitle (optional) and author’s name and be in RGB colour mode for ideal screen viewing. Cover art should NOT include any adverts, sale stickers, or price tags. Cover art must NOT infringe on another publisher’s or artist’s copyright. If asked you must be able to present proof that you have a legal right to use the images on your book’s cover. Any artwork with advertisements, price tags or stickers will be rejected for publication.

Uploading Book Details

For every book you upload for sale, you will be asked to provide detailed information, including the book’s genre, author, publisher, editor, ISBN #, keywords, pricing, copyright information, date of publication, language, region, manuscript, cover art, payment information, etc. Fields with an asterisk (*) are required for us to publish your book to our site. You can save your progress as you upload your book and its details and come back to it anytime. Once you have completed all of the necessary fields, you can submit your book for publication. AfricanBooks.com will then review your submission and either mark it for publishing or respond to you with any needed changes before publishing. In general, it takes 24 to 48 hours to publish your book to our site.

Updating Book Details

Sometimes you may find a need to update the book details you provided about one of your books. In this case, you can return to your bookshelf and make the required changes. Your changes will automatically be applied to our store for potential readers to see.

Updating Your Book Manuscript

In the case where the manuscript of your book must be updated, you can submit a manuscript update, attach the file and upload the manuscript. You will also include a small brief on what was updated in the manuscript for our easy review and reference. This is particularly important if you are adding new content to the manuscript verses simply making spelling and/or grammar adjustments.

We encourage authors wishing to make major manuscript changes, to consider producing a ‘2nd edition’ book rather than simply updating the book manuscript as these changes will affect people who have already purchased your ‘1st edition.’

You can find more information on Multi-Edition Books, below. Please note, that in most cases, changes to your book’s manuscript will only affect future buyers and not past sales. Readers who already purchased your book, will still have access to the original manuscript they received upon initial purchase. It is therefore very important to make sure that you have proofread and edited your manuscript for production before submitting it for publication on AfricanBooks.com

Updating Cover Art

When you upload your cover art, you will be able to preview the art uploaded. It is important for you to make sure that the artwork is in proper alignment, clear and cropped appropriately. If changes are needed, simply delete the cover you uploaded and re-upload the cover again with the necessary changes.

If you update your book cover art, you can also submit it to us through the book portal, indicating the updated artwork and reason for the change. If major changes are made to both the cover and interior file, we encourage you to consider publishing your book as a 2nd edition rather than simply updating your first edition. More information is included below.

Please note, that in most cases, changes to your book’s cover art will only affect future buyers and not past sales. Readers who already purchased your book, will still have access to the original art covers they received upon initial purchase.

Multi-Edition Books

We are sure you have heard of 1st, 2nd and 3rd editions of books. Over time, authors find the need to update their books to stay relevant with current trends or times, or to fix a change of mindsets, beliefs and/or opinions originally shared. For books that have major content changes, above and beyond simple grammar and spelling updates, we encourage authors to upload a “NEW BOOK” rather than “UPDATING THEIR BOOK.”

By listing your new book upload as a 2nd edition, you encourage readers who purchased the 1st edition to return and buy the updated 2nd edition. This spurs on additional sales from your already loyal reading base. Authors who want to discontinue the sales of the 1st edition and only offer 2nd editions can do so by ‘unpublishing’ the 1st edition. Readers who purchased the original editions, will still have access to their purchased content, but new buyers will not be able to purchase ‘unpublished’ content and will therefore only be able to buy the new edition of your book.

If, however you want readers to be able to purchase multiple editions of your book at the same time, you can keep both your 1st and 2nd editions available for purchase. In these cases however, it is important that you make major changes to the content and/or cover so that readers find value in purchasing both versions of your book.

Unpublishing & Deleting Books

At times you may find it necessary to unpublish or remove your book from our publishing platform. In these instances, you will submit an unpublishing or deletion request through your bookshelf. This will hide your book from future buyers on our site. This however, will not delete your book from reader libraries who have already purchased your book. Changes to your books, will only affect future sales and not past sales.

Keywords & Genres

As an author it is important for you to categorize your books accurately. Often times readers search for keywords and genres when looking for new books to purchase. Your selection of keywords and genres will ensure that your book is easily found by those looking for it.

GENRE Selection. Our site has set genre categories in which books can be listed. When uploading a new book, you must select a ‘Main’ category or genre in which your book can be categorized. If your book falls in two genres or categories, you can also select a secondary, or sub-category. The secondary category is optional however, and does not need to be specified if it does not apply to your specific book.

KEYWORDS. When uploading a new book, an author has the option to type in up to 7 keywords that accurately describe their book. For example, if an author uploads a book about vision and purpose for young people, they may type in words like: PURPOSE, CALLING, VISION, MOTIVATIONAL, YOUTH, LIFE-PLANNING, MY FUTURE.
