Pricing Your Book

It is important for you to price your book for success. At AfricanBooks.com we give you complete freedom over your pricing. That being said, we have recommended price points that we know help you sell your eBooks faster. Traditionally, paperback books in the African setting sell for approximately $6 - $8 (That is between 20-30,000/= Ugandan Shillings). These paperback books cost money to print and distribute.

eBooks on the other hand, have very little upfront costs and can be sold for less while still earning authors a good return on their intellectual property investments. As an e-commerce eBook platform, we have noticed that eBooks priced between $2 and $4.15 sell the most, and are price-friendly within the African eBook market. We also noticed that the higher the eBook is priced at, the fewer sales are made, with people opting for print versions over eBook versions at higher price points. It is with this data and knowledge that we ask you to carefully consider pricing your book.

To help encourage authors and publishers to price their books for success, we reward writers with larger royalty returns when they price their books within specific ranges, meaning that authors make more money when they price their books at a lower price point than when they price it at a higher price point.

We have 4 different payment plans for publishers to choose from, so you can choose what works best for you. The great thing is that you have the freedom to choose your Payment Plan with each book you upload and you can change your plan as needed, if you find sales are not moving as quickly as you expected.

Sales Reports

Your dashboard provides sales reports for each of your published books. These reports keep you abreast on your book’s earnings and allow you to make decisions on whether or not your book is priced for maximum success. Often times, books in higher price ranges will sell less frequently, while lower priced books will sell much faster. Use your sales reports to track such sales, also monitor the success of your book sales, book launches and other marketing strategies.

Royalties

In addition to tracking your sales and monitoring sales reports, you can also track your earnings/royalties in your dashboard. There you will find payout history, earnings history and much more. It is important that in this section of your dashboard you keep your payment details updated to ensure that your royalties can be paid out on time.

Banking

To ensure the timely distribution of your royalties, we ask all authors and publishers to include their banking details, identification, tax information and contact details for each book they upload. These bank details can be updated at any time from your dashboard. You are responsible for giving accurate payout account information to ensure you receive your royalties.

At the moment, AfricanBooks.com can distribute your royalty payments to your Bank or to your registered Mobile Money account. The accounts we pay to must have your name or your publishing entity’s legal name as the account holder listed.

Taxes

AfricanBooks.com requires all authors and publishers, including nonprofit or tax-exempt organizations, to provide valid taxpayer identification in order to comply with all tax reporting regulations. Tax withholding and specific requirements vary based on your country of origin, residency status and tax status. In order to sell on our platform, you must provide your national identification number, your tax identification number and any country specific tax requirements specified by your country of origin.

AfricanBooks.com reserves the right to verify the tax information provided to us, and will report royalty earnings paid out to you as required by law. AfricanBooks.com is not required to withhold taxes from your royalty earnings once you have provided us with verified tax information unless we are otherwise notified to do so by law.

Please note that you are responsible for submitting your state or country specific income taxes to your tax authority. In general, royalty payments for sales are subject to 30% tax withholding but this may vary by country. It is important that you submit your taxes as you are required by law so as not to incur any tax penalties from your tax authority.

Also note, that your tax authority and corresponding tax identification number should be issued from the same country as your corresponding bank account specified for royalty payments. For example, royalties paid to a US Bank account will be subject to US Tax Law. Similarly, royalties paid to Ugandan Bank accounts will be subject to Ugandan Tax Law.

UJAAMA

Wealth Building Plan

In this plan both authors (65%) and publishers (5%) earn MAXIMIZED Royalties but with limited flexibility in book pricing. Authors and publishers seeking the highest earnings per book should price their books between $2 - $4.15 (or 7,000/= to 14,500/= Ugandan Shillings). We recommend authors and publishers to take advantage of this plan because it prices your book for more sales and gives you greater returns on each book you distribute.
UHURU

Freedom Plan

Similar to the 55% plan, this plan offers higher royalty options but with reduced flexibility in book costing. In this plan authors earn 51% royalties, with publishers earning 4% on every book sold. To take advantage of this plan, you must price your book between $4.16 - $5.49 (That’s 15k-19k /= Ugandan Shillings)
UMOJA

Unity Plan

This plan, similar to the 45% plan, offers publishers Mid-Range Royalties while giving them higher book-cost flexibility when pricing their author’s books. In this plan, authors earn a 42% royalty on every book sold, while publishers earn a 3% royalty on every sale. To take advantage of this plan, you must price your book between $5.50 - $6 (That is 19,500/= to 21,000/= in Ugandan Shillings)
UBUNTU

Equality Plan

This plan, similar to the 45% plan, offers publishers Mid-Range Royalties while giving them higher book-cost flexibility when pricing their author’s books. In this plan, authors earn a 42% royalty on every book sold, while publishers earn a 3% royalty on every sale. To take advantage of this plan, you must price your book between $5.50 - $6 (That is 19,500/= to 21,000/= in Ugandan Shillings)
